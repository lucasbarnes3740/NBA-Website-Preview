About Us

Local captains. Family-run. Built on a love of the water.

How It Started

At Home on
Narragansett Bay

Captain Nik and his brother Lucas spent their summers on Narragansett Bay — surfing, fishing, and taking anyone they could out on the family's old Boston Whaler. What started as a passion became a mission: to share that same authentic, unfiltered joy of the ocean with everyone who steps aboard.

From your first contact with us to your final step off the boat, our goal is to make every part of the experience seamless, personal, and enjoyable. We care deeply about great service, going the extra mile, and creating lasting memories that keep our guests coming back year after year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before setting out.

01
Booking & Pricing
In the event of a cancellation, you will receive a full refund if you cancel more than 7 days before the charter, a 50% refund if you cancel 2–7 days before, and no refund if you cancel within 48 hours.
We recommend booking 2–4 weeks in advance, especially during peak season (June–August). Weekends and holidays fill quickly. Last-minute bookings may be available, so contact us directly to check.
02
The Boat & Capacity
The 33’ Grady White One Day is USCG-rated for up to 12 passengers, making it a great option for families, friends, or small groups. With plenty of room to relax and move around, it offers a spacious and comfortable experience on the water.
Yes — USCG-approved life jackets in all sizes (including children’s) are provided on board and are required to be accessible at all times.
Yes — One Day does have an enclosed head (bathroom) onboard for guest use. On longer charters, we're happy to stop near additional facilities if needed. As with most boats, the restroom is compact, but available for your convenience.
Absolutely — swimming is one of the best parts of a charter! We’ll find a calm, beautiful spot to drop anchor so you can jump in. Let us know when booking if a swim stop is a priority and we’ll plan around it.
03
Food, Drinks & Add-Ons
Absolutely — you're welcome to bring your own food, beverages, and alcohol for personal consumption. We have a cooler onboard with light refreshments, and also offer catering options through local partners.
We’ve partnered with some of Newport’s best: our own charcuterie boards, pizza from Imbriglia’s, sandwiches from Newport Sandwich Company, and award-winning lobster rolls and clam chowder from Newport Chowder Company. Add any of these when booking.
Yes, guests are welcome to bring and consume alcohol responsibly. The captain reserves the right to limit alcohol consumption if safety is a concern. Please drink responsibly and stay hydrated — the sun on the water is strong!
04
Weather & Safety
If unfavorable weather is expected, we will proactively contact you as early as possible to discuss potential rescheduling or cancellation. If your booking is canceled, you will receive a full refund.
Children are welcome! Life jackets in all sizes including children’s are provided. We recommend children wear sunscreen and be comfortable near water. Please let us know the ages of any children when booking.
05
Logistics & Departure
Our main pickup location is Ann Street Pier on Thames St. We also offer pickup at Perrotti Park, Fort Adams, Goat Island, and Jamestown. If you'd prefer another location, reach out and we'll do our best to accommodate.
We recommend layers (it’s cooler on the water), non-slip shoes, sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat. If you plan to swim, bring a towel and change of clothes. A light jacket is always a good idea even in summer.
We operate seasonally from May through October, with peak season running from June through September. Availability in the early and late season may vary depending on weather and scheduling.
Yes! We offer a full-day Block Island package as well as full-day trips to explore local destinations. If you have a specific spot in mind, reach out and we'd be happy to discuss options. Fuel charges may apply depending on the distance.
06
Large Groups (7–12 Guests) — How It Works

For groups of 7–12, U.S. Coast Guard regulations require us to operate as a Bareboat Charter — meaning you rent the vessel and select your own captain. Groups of 1–6 have a captain automatically included.

Captain Options

  • Our Captains (recommended) — Choose Phillip Amason or Austin Kennedy. The easiest option: service & fuel fees are paid directly to them on arrival.
  • Bring Your Own Captain — Must meet minimum requirements and be approved in advance.
  • Operate Yourself — Must meet minimum requirements and provide documentation in advance.

A captain must be designated before departure — the vessel will not depart without one.

Minimum Captain Requirements

  • USCG Masters License
  • 100+ days of experience on Narragansett Bay / Newport Harbor
  • $2M liability insurance covering the vessel, Newport Boat Adventures, and the marina

Payment

Boat rental is paid to Newport Boat Adventures at booking. Captain service & fuel fees are paid directly to your captain, separately from the charter fee.
